About Next Wave Cancer Rehab

Move Forward With Confidence

Cancer treatment can leave people feeling uncertain about their body — how it moves, how it responds, and what it needs next. Next Wave Cancer Rehab was founded to help people safely rebuild strength, restore movement, and regain confidence at every stage of their cancer experience.

Whether you are preparing for surgery, recovering from treatment, navigating long-term side effects, or living with advanced illness, Megan Oster provides safe, individualised physiotherapy care that meets you where you are.

Behind the Name

The name Next Wave Cancer Rehab reflects something that anyone who has experienced cancer, or supported someone through it, understands deeply.

Recovery rarely moves in a straight line. It comes in waves. Some are large and overwhelming. Others are smaller, steadier, and easier to meet. Learning to read the waves, and to move with them rather than against them, is part of what cancer rehabilitation is about.

The Sunshine Coast is home to Next Wave Cancer Rehab, and the ocean is woven into its identity, not as a cliché, but as a genuine reference to rhythm, resilience, change, and renewal.

The name is also a personal tribute. It honours Megan's Mum, and the memory of sitting together and waiting for the next wave.

Meet Megan

Founder and Director, Oncology Physiotherapist, Upper Quadrant Lymphoedema-Trained Physiotherapist and Clinical Pilates Instructor

I established Next Wave Cancer Rehab because I wanted to offer people affected by cancer something I had seen make a real difference: thoughtful, experienced, oncology-informed physiotherapy care, delivered in a calm and private setting.

My background spans over a decade of physiotherapy practice across Australia and the United Kingdom, with a focus on oncology rehabilitation, musculoskeletal physiotherapy, and supportive cancer care.

I understand how uncertain the body can feel during and after cancer treatment. My role is to help you understand what is happening, what is safe, and how to move forward at your pace, and in collaboration with your treating team.

Megan holds formal training in upper quadrant lymphoedema management. She uses this training to support early identification, risk reduction, and basic management strategies, and works closely with dedicated lymphoedema therapists when more comprehensive care is required.

Career and Experience

Megan has worked across some of Australia's and the United Kingdom's leading oncology and hospital settings, building a clinical background that spans complex cancer presentations, surgical rehabilitation, supportive care, and multidisciplinary teamwork.

  • The Royal Marsden Hospital, London

    One of the world's foremost cancer centres, where Megan worked alongside specialist oncology teams on complex surgical and medical presentations.

  • Princess Alexandra Hospital, Brisbane

    Tertiary oncology and complex surgical care, including multidisciplinary team collaboration and patient education.

  • Cancer Council Queensland

    Supportive cancer care, patient education, and community-based rehabilitation support.

  • Senior Supportive Care Consultant

    Service development, clinical leadership, quality improvement, and mentoring within oncology rehabilitation.

  • Private Practice

    Oncology and musculoskeletal physiotherapy, working with people across diagnosis, treatment, survivorship, and advanced illness.

My Approach

I believe rehabilitation works best when it starts early and stays responsive. Whether someone is preparing for surgery, managing the effects of treatment, or finding their way after cancer, my aim is to offer clear guidance, honest conversation, and a plan that can adapt as things change.
