Move Forward With Confidence

Cancer treatment can leave people unsure of their body — how it moves, how it responds, and what it needs next. Side effects, surgery, and the emotional weight of diagnosis can make even everyday movement feel uncertain.

Next Wave Cancer Rehab helps people safely rebuild strength, restore movement, and regain confidence at every stage of their cancer experience. Care is individualised, evidence-informed, and delivered at your pace, in collaboration with your medical team.

Behind The Name

I chose the name Next Wave because cancer recovery is rarely linear. It moves in waves — some large and overwhelming, others small and steady. That rhythm is something many people recognise in their own experience.

Living on the Sunshine Coast, the ocean is part of everyday life here. It represents resilience, change, and renewal — qualities that resonate with rehabilitation and healing, without needing to be literal or decorative.

The name is also deeply personal. It is a tribute to my Mum, and to the memory of waiting for the next wave together. That sense of patience, presence, and quiet hope is at the heart of how I work with people.

Meet Megan

Megan Oster

Founder and Director

  • Oncology physiotherapist
  • Upper quadrant lymphoedema-trained
  • Clinical Pilates instructor
  • Sunshine Coast-based

My role is to guide you through uncertainty — helping you rebuild strength, restore movement, and feel more confident in your body again. I bring together oncology physiotherapy, musculoskeletal rehabilitation, and Clinical Pilates to support care that meets you where you are.

Whether you are preparing for treatment, recovering after surgery, managing side effects, or navigating survivorship, I aim to provide clear guidance, calm reassurance, and a plan that respects your goals and your treating team.

I hold formal training in upper quadrant lymphoedema management. I use this training to support early identification, risk reduction, and basic management strategies, and work closely with dedicated lymphoedema therapists when more comprehensive care is required.

Career & Experience

Megan brings more than a decade of physiotherapy experience across oncology and musculoskeletal rehabilitation, in Australia and the United Kingdom.

Her clinical background includes work at The Royal Marsden Hospital in London and Princess Alexandra Hospital in Brisbane, alongside private practice, Cancer Council Queensland, and a Senior Supportive Care Consultant role. This breadth of experience supports care that is clinically credible, compassionate, and practical.

  • Oncology and musculoskeletal rehabilitation
  • Multidisciplinary and collaborative care
  • Patient education and supportive care
  • Service development and clinical leadership
  • Quality improvement and mentoring
