# Brand Style Guide

# Brand Positioning

## Brand Personality

Next Wave Cancer Rehab should feel like a calm, experienced clinician sitting beside a patient and helping them understand the next step.

The brand should feel:

- calm
- grounded
- clinically trustworthy
- warm
- compassionate
- steady
- mature
- human
- professional
- hopeful without being overly cheerful
- premium without feeling corporate
- local without feeling parochial

## Brand Meaning

The name **Next Wave** reflects several layers:

1. Cancer recovery is rarely linear. It moves in waves: some large and overwhelming, others small and steady.
2. The Sunshine Coast location gives the brand a natural coastal influence.
3. The ocean represents rhythm, resilience, change, and renewal.
4. The idea of “waves” extends beyond geography and represents recovery, adaptation, and healing.
5. The name is personally meaningful to Megan and is a tribute to her Mum and the memory of waiting for the next wave together.

The design should reference waves subtly through rhythm, flow, spacing, curves, and gentle visual movement. It should not look like a literal beach resort or wellness retreat.

# Brand Voice and Copy Tone

## Voice

The website voice should be:

- calm
- clear
- warm
- clinically credible
- respectful
- emotionally safe
- reassuring
- steady
- plain-English
- supportive without being patronising

The copy should be written for people who may be anxious, fatigued, overwhelmed, or unsure what is safe.

## Use Language Like

- Move forward with confidence
- Safe, individualised support
- Clear guidance
- At your pace
- The right guidance, at the right time
- Rebuild strength
- Restore movement
- Regain confidence
- Feel supported, informed, and in control
- You do not have to navigate this alone
- A plan that meets you where you are
- Support through treatment, recovery, survivorship, and advanced illness
- In collaboration with your medical team

## Avoid Language Like

- beat cancer
- fight cancer
- cancer warrior
- cure
- guarantee
- eliminate
- miracle
- transformation
- life-changing results
- best
- no risk
- fear-based sales language
- overly cheerful wellness language
- excessive use of the word “journey”
- “specialist” unless legally and professionally appropriate

## Clinical Claim Style

Use careful qualifiers:

- may help
- can support
- aims to
- where appropriate
- depending on your treatment and goals
- in many cases
- in collaboration with your treating team
- individualised to your needs

Avoid absolute promises.

# Logo and Visual Identity

## Current Logo

The current logo includes:

- a circular wave / ocean symbol
- the text “Next Wave Cancer Rehab”
- the tagline: **Guided by expertise. Driven by care.**
- a warm coastal palette using green, teal, sand, and clay tones

`assets/logo-reference-final.png`

## Logo Usage Notes

- Use the full logo in the header if it remains readable at smaller sizes.
- A flat transparent version may be needed for production.
- Consider using the logo symbol separately as a favicon or subtle brand motif.
- Do not overuse the wave symbol as decoration.
- Keep the header clean and calm.
- Confirm whether the tagline should appear in the header, hero section, footer, or only inside the logo asset.

# Colour Palette

The confirmed colour palette is:

| Token | Name | Hex | Intended Use |
|---|---|---:|---|
| `--color-primary-sage` | Soft Eucalyptus Sage | `#A8BFB3` | Main brand accents, lighter UI elements, soft highlights |
| `--color-primary-dark` | Deep Eucalyptus | `#3F665C` | Primary buttons, structural elements, strong brand presence |
| `--color-background` | Warm Off-White | `#F7F4EF` | Main page background |
| `--color-warm-neutral` | Warm Sand | `#D8C3A5` | Borders, secondary containers, subtle depth |
| `--color-text` | Deep Charcoal Green | `#24332F` | Body text, headings, high-contrast interface text |
| `--color-accent` | Soft Clay Rose | `#C9A79C` | Special highlights, small accents, occasional CTA support |

## Colour Personality

The palette should feel:

- warm
- soft
- organic
- grounded
- calm
- coastal without being beachy
- clinical without being sterile
- premium without being cold

## Recommended Colour Usage

### Background

Use Warm Off-White `#F7F4EF` as the main page background.

### Text

Use Deep Charcoal Green `#24332F` for body text and headings.

### Buttons

Use Deep Eucalyptus `#3F665C` for primary buttons.

Button text should generally be white or warm off-white for contrast.

### Accents

Use Soft Eucalyptus Sage `#A8BFB3` for subtle cards, highlights, section details, badges, and background tints.

Use Warm Sand `#D8C3A5` for dividers, soft borders, and secondary containers.

Use Soft Clay Rose `#C9A79C` sparingly. It should not dominate the brand. It can be used for small highlights, gentle emphasis, or supporting accent areas.

## Colour Accessibility Notes

Recommended combinations:

- Deep Charcoal Green on Warm Off-White
- White or Warm Off-White text on Deep Eucalyptus
- Deep Charcoal Green text on Soft Eucalyptus Sage
- Deep Charcoal Green text on Warm Sand
- Deep Charcoal Green text on Soft Clay Rose

Avoid:

- white text on Soft Eucalyptus Sage
- white text on Soft Clay Rose
- pale text on pale backgrounds
- decorative text with poor contrast
- long blocks of text in sage, sand, or rose

# Typography Direction

Typography should be:

- highly readable
- calm
- mature
- accessible for older adults
- suitable for people experiencing fatigue or cognitive load
- premium without being decorative

## Recommended Starting Point

Use system fonts initially for simplicity:

- Headings: Georgia, Cambria, Times New Roman, serif
- Body: system-ui, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, Segoe UI, sans-serif

This keeps the site simple and fast.

## Future Typography Option

If Google Fonts are added later, possible pairings include:

- Source Serif 4 + Source Sans 3
- Fraunces + Inter
- Lora + Source Sans 3
- Cormorant Garamond + Inter, used carefully
- Manrope only, for an all-sans premium healthcare feel

Do not add Google Fonts until the core layout is stable.

# Imagery Direction

Imagery should feel:

- real
- calm
- private
- supportive
- professional
- grounded
- Sunshine Coast-connected
- human
- non-performative

## Good Image Types

- Megan professional portrait
- calm clinic details
- hands-on care details, with consent
- subtle coastal textures
- soft horizon / wave / light imagery
- gentle movement imagery
- walking, mobility, strength, confidence
- quiet treatment-room imagery
- abstract coastal forms

## Avoid

- generic cancer stock imagery
- overly happy staged patient photos
- hospital-cold imagery
- strong pink ribbon imagery
- beach resort clichés
- wellness retreat clichés
- overly clinical equipment-heavy photos
- before-and-after imagery
- emotionally manipulative cancer imagery
