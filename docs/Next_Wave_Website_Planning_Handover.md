# 1. Project Overview

Next Wave Cancer Rehab is a calm, premium, clinically credible healthcare website for a Sunshine Coast oncology physiotherapy service.

The website should help people affected by cancer understand:

- what cancer rehabilitation is
- who the service supports
- when they can access care
- what services Megan provides
- what to expect at the first appointment
- how to make contact
- how medical referrers can refer patients

The website must also communicate local trust, clinical experience, compassion, and safety.

The core emotional message of the website is:

> You are safe here. You are understood. There is a clear next step.

The website should reduce uncertainty for people who may feel overwhelmed, fatigued, anxious, physically vulnerable, or unsure what movement and rehabilitation are safe after diagnosis, treatment, surgery, or cancer-related side effects.

# 5. Brand Positioning

## Brand Personality

Next Wave Cancer Rehab should feel like a calm, experienced clinician sitting beside a patient and helping them understand the next step.

The brand should feel:

- calm
- grounded
- clinically trustworthy
- warm
- compassionate
- steady
- mature
- human
- professional
- hopeful without being overly cheerful
- premium without feeling corporate
- local without feeling parochial

## Brand Meaning

The name **Next Wave** reflects several layers:

1. Cancer recovery is rarely linear. It moves in waves: some large and overwhelming, others small and steady.
2. The Sunshine Coast location gives the brand a natural coastal influence.
3. The ocean represents rhythm, resilience, change, and renewal.
4. The idea of “waves” extends beyond geography and represents recovery, adaptation, and healing.
5. The name is personally meaningful to Megan and is a tribute to her Mum and the memory of waiting for the next wave together.

The design should reference waves subtly through rhythm, flow, spacing, curves, and gentle visual movement. It should not look like a literal beach resort or wellness retreat.

- restore shoulder, chest wall, trunk, and joint mobility
- support return to everyday activity, work, exercise, and hobbies
- manage pain, stiffness, and post-operative discomfort
- improve strength, fitness, and function
- support scar healing and scar management
- support surgery-specific side effects such as cording
- monitor lymphoedema risk and early signs
- improve posture and movement patterns
- reduce the impact of fatigue
- provide support garment guidance
- provide reassurance and a clear recovery plan

## Key Message

Cancer rehabilitation is not only for after treatment. It can support and guide people at any stage of their cancer experience, from diagnosis through treatment, survivorship, and advanced illness.

Next Wave Cancer Rehab works with all tumour groups, with patients beyond adolescent years.

## Preventative Rehabilitation

Position as:

Getting ahead of treatment.

Include:

- soon after diagnosis
- preparing for treatment
- maintaining physical function
- reducing treatment side effects before they become significant
- pre-surgery assessment and planning

## Restorative Rehabilitation

Position as:

Helping people regain strength, movement, confidence, and independence after cancer treatment, surgery, or illness.

## Survivorship Rehabilitation

Position as:

Helping people bridge the gap between treatment and life after cancer.

Include:

- fatigue
- reduced strength
- pain
- stiffness
- loss of confidence
- uncertainty about what the body can handle
- returning to work, exercise, family life, travel, and meaningful activity

Important line:

Cancer may be part of your story, but it does not have to define what comes next.

## Palliative Rehabilitation

Position as:

Maintaining comfort, function, independence where possible, and quality of life for people living with advanced or incurable cancer.

Tone must be gentle, respectful, and non-promotional.

## First Appointment

Include:

- no referral required
- patients can get in touch by phone, email, or contact form
- initial consultation is in person and 60 minutes
- discussion of diagnosis, treatments received or planned, side effects, and goals
- pre-appointment intake form
- oncology team may complete part of intake form where possible
- consultation takes place in a private and comfortable environment
- wear comfortable clothing
- bring hospital exercise booklets, post-operative garments, scans, reports, or referral letters if relevant
- goal is clarity, reassurance, and a plan

## Telehealth

Position as:

Megan primarily provides face-to-face appointments on the Sunshine Coast, with telehealth available where appropriate.

Telehealth can help improve access for people in rural and regional communities who may not have experienced oncology rehabilitation support nearby.

# 17. Accessibility Requirements

Aim for WCAG 2.2 AA where practical.

## Key Requirements

- semantic HTML
- clear heading hierarchy
- body text at least 16px, preferably 17–18px where appropriate
- strong colour contrast
- visible focus states
- keyboard navigable menus and forms
- descriptive link text
- large tap targets
- clear form labels
- helpful error messages
- alt text for meaningful images
- decorative images marked appropriately
- no important text embedded only in images
- no auto-playing carousels
- no excessive animation
- respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- mobile-first responsive design
- avoid low-contrast text
- avoid placing text over busy images

## Healthcare Accessibility Considerations

Users may be:

- fatigued
- anxious
- older
- cognitively overloaded
- experiencing pain
- recovering from surgery
- using assistive technology
- searching on mobile while under stress

Therefore:

- keep pages scannable
- use short paragraphs
- use clear section headings
- provide anchor links for long pages
- make CTAs easy to find
- make contact information obvious
- avoid unnecessary interaction patterns
