Privacy Policy

Your Privacy Matters to Us

Effective July 2026

At Next Wave Cancer Rehab, we are committed to protecting your personal and health information and handling it with care, respect, and transparency. This policy explains what we collect, how we use it, and how you can access or correct your information.

Our Commitment to Your Privacy

We comply with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs), and other applicable Australian privacy legislation. We understand that the information you share with us is personal and often sensitive, and we are committed to keeping it secure and confidential.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, how we collect and use it, when and why we may share it, how we protect it, how you can access or correct it, and how to make a privacy complaint.

Next Wave Cancer Rehab (ABN 30 964 656 284) is a sole trader business operated by Megan Kate Oster, Principal Physiotherapist and Founder.

What Personal Information We Collect

  • Personal information, including your name, date of birth, address, phone number, email address, and emergency contact details.
  • Health information, including your medical history, cancer diagnosis and treatment history, medications, allergies, imaging and pathology reports, referrals, physiotherapy assessments, exercise programs, clinical notes, and appointment history.
  • Billing and administrative information, including Medicare, DVA, and private health insurance details, your Healthcare Identifier (IHI), and payment and invoice information.

How and Why We Use Your Information

How We Collect Your Information

We generally collect information directly from you, through appointments, registration forms, telephone, email, or our website. With your consent, or where permitted by law, we may also collect information from your GP, specialists, hospitals, allied health professionals, pathology and imaging providers, Medicare, DVA, and private health insurers.

Why We Collect Your Information

We collect information to provide safe, evidence-informed physiotherapy care, develop personalised rehabilitation programs, communicate with your treating team, coordinate your care, process payments and claims, manage appointments, maintain accurate records, and comply with our legal and professional obligations.

Who May We Share Your Information With

Where appropriate, and with your consent or where required by law, we may share your information with members of your treating team, hospitals, Medicare, DVA, HICAPS, private health insurers, pathology and imaging providers, and relevant regulatory authorities.

Electronic Communication

We may communicate with you by email, telephone, SMS reminders, and secure electronic health systems. While we take reasonable steps to protect your information, no method of electronic communication can be guaranteed completely secure. Please let us know if you would prefer not to receive electronic communications.

Website and Online Privacy

When you visit our website, limited technical information, such as your IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, and website usage statistics, may be collected to help us improve website performance and security. This information is generally anonymous.

Online Enquiries

If you submit an enquiry or referral through our website, we collect the information you choose to provide, such as your name, email address, phone number, and message, solely to respond to your enquiry or assist with arranging an appointment. This information is stored within our own Google Workspace environment (Google Sheets and Gmail) rather than a third-party form or marketing platform, and is not used for marketing without your consent.

Analytics and Cookies

We do not currently use analytics or tracking cookies on this website. If this changes in future, for example through the introduction of a tool such as Google Analytics, we will update this policy in advance to reflect it.

Keeping Your Information Secure

Security of Your Information

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information. Clinical records are held in our secure, cloud-based practice management software (Cliniko), with password-protected access and encrypted data transmission. Enquiries and referrals submitted through our website are stored within our own Google Workspace environment (Google Sheets and Gmail), rather than a third-party form or marketing platform. Across both systems, we maintain restricted access to records, regular software updates, and secure disposal of confidential information.

Overseas Disclosure

Next Wave Cancer Rehab does not routinely disclose your information overseas. Some of our secure cloud service providers may store encrypted data on overseas servers as part of their infrastructure. Where this occurs, appropriate privacy safeguards apply.

How Long We Keep Your Information

In line with our professional and legal obligations, we retain patient health records for a minimum of seven years from the date of your last contact with our practice. Some records may need to be retained for longer where required by law.

Your Rights and Choices

Accessing and Correcting Your Information

You may request access to, or correction of, your personal information by contacting us in writing. We aim to respond within 30 days. A reasonable administrative fee may apply for extensive requests.

Receiving Care Anonymously

Where lawful and practical, you may receive services anonymously or under a pseudonym. Given the nature of physiotherapy and the need to communicate with your treating team, this may not always be possible.

Our Patients

Next Wave Cancer Rehab provides care to adults aged 20 years and over. We do not provide care to children or adolescents.

Privacy Complaints

If you have concerns about your privacy, please contact us first. We aim to respond within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

This policy may be updated from time to time to reflect changes in legislation, professional standards, technology, or our services. The latest version will always be available on our website.
